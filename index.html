A note from Eduba · April 2026

Science meets strategy meets a methodology question.

October 2025. QCC and the Florida Opportunity Fund sign an MOU for co-investment in quantum technologies. December 3, 2025. Florida Quantum launches at Tech Basel Miami AI Summit, co-led with Medina Ventures. February 26, 2026. A QCC senior advisor role announced at The Quantum Insider.

This note addresses one question inside the fund's thesis: how to think about orchestration across classical and quantum compute, at the moment a portfolio company has to decide which layer a given problem belongs on.

01 · The frame

Most early-stage quantum companies are classical companies with a quantum kernel inside.

A practical view of where AI, rule-based logic, and traditional code fit inside a quantum-adjacent startup. The methodology for deciding which layer each piece of a new workflow belongs on is directly useful to every QCC portfolio company and currently not published anywhere the fund can point to.

60%
Classical code & database
Control plane, data pipelines, simulation harnesses, developer tooling, interfaces for classical partners.
30%
Rule-based logic
Error-correction heuristics. Qubit-scheduling rules. Compliance, governance, and post-quantum cryptography workflows.
10%
Genuine AI or quantum work
Classical ML on quantum output. The quantum kernel itself. Hybrid classical-quantum orchestration.
02 · The closest precedent

Feeld.

Scoped sprint for a small product team whose technical founder wanted a methodology the team could keep running after the engagement closed. Deliverables: workshop, advisory calls, Organizational Context Architecture, Strategic Operations Framework.

The fit to a QCC GP engagement is a matter of swapping vocabulary. Organizational Context Architecture becomes the playbook QCC hands to portfolio-company CTOs on close. Strategic Operations Framework becomes the Florida Quantum convening's methodology surface.

03 · The underlying work

Interpretable Context Methodology.

Submitted to ACM TiiS. Agent and compute context organized as a layered filesystem with measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains. Natural companion to the hybrid classical-quantum orchestration question QCC portfolio companies will run into. Open source under MIT license.
